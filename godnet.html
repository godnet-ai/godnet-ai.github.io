What is ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE?
The intelligence is shown by a machine to learn and think just like humans do.
To understand this let’s imagine a scenario. You are having a conversation with your superior, who has a bigger vocabulary. While talking, he said a word, ‘jabber’, which you have heard for the first time. Well, just like me, English is not your native language and you don’t want to look stupid in front of him. So you decided to understand what the word means through the context and kept talking. Since you are smart, after some time you figured out that a ‘jabber’ means to talk noisily.

Can we make machines do the same thing? It turns out, yes. A team of researchers Google developed a group of related models called Word2Vec which does something pretty similar. Using Word2Vec, a machine can tell what might ‘jabber’ means.

Artificial Intelligence or AI, deals with the art and science of making machines that can do tasks that only “intelligent humans” are supposed to do. Like playing chess, driving a car and telling the meaning of a word just from the context


What is a Neural Network(NN)?

Remember the simple function we talked about: f (x) = 2x² + 3x + 1 = y. Here the input x goes through a transformation and produces y. Let’s call this one layer of computation. What if we apply some other transformation on this output y and convert it into some z. Thus the overall transformation that x went through to produce z is much more complex than the transformation required to produce y.

NN pretty much follows the same concept. It’s are just a bunch of nodes connected layer by layer which defines the transformation that the input would go through to produce the output. A specific connection (or network) of layers and nodes is called an Architecture.

Neural Networks: Nodes and layers define the rules to turn inputs to outputs

Each node has some ‘parameters’ associated with it, which are used to perform the transformations. These parameters are learned using some past data (called training data) and a learning algorithm (like Gradient Descent). Just like you would adjust the pegs to tune the string of a guitar until you hit the perfect note, we adjust the ‘parameters’ of a neural network until it has learned how to perform the task that you want it to perform.


Convolutional Neural Networks— CNNs


The above shown neural network is known as a ‘Multi-Layer Perceptron’. However, there are other variants of neural networks that are suited for specific tasks. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are one such class of neural networks that are most commonly applied to analyze visual imagery.
CNN's work by extracting a more and more abstract representation of the features in an image by passing the image through convolutional layers. This representation is learned by adjusting the parameters associated with the layers such that it satisfies some given training data for the task.

There are two main classifications of chaos.
  1. First Order Chaos doesn’t respond to prediction. If you predict the weather to some level of accuracy, that prediction will hold because the weather doesn’t adjust based on the prediction itself.
  2. Second-Order Chaos is infinitely less predictable because it does respond to prediction. Examples include things like stocks and politics.
